## Admission Eligibility

The fellowship is for final-year IT students and CS graduates/professionals with math, programming, and Python skills.

To be eligible for the AI Fellowship program, applicants must meet the following requirements:

**Academic Background:**
- Final year IT/CS students, recent graduates, or working professionals with computer science background
- Completed courses in linear algebra, probability, statistics, and basic calculus
- Completed programming and computer science courses

**Technical Skills:**
- Proficiency in Python programming
- Strong foundation in computer science fundamentals

**Soft Skills:**
- Proficiency in English language
- Ability to work collaboratively with others
- Strong communication skills
- Positive learning attitude and self-motivation

## Admission Process

The admission process involves: online application, document submission, entrance exam, interview, and email confirmation.

The admission process typically follows these steps:

1. **Online Application:** Fill out the complete online application form with your personal and academic details
2. **Document Submission:** Submit required academic documents (transcripts, certificates, etc.)
3. **Entrance Examination:** Appear for the entrance exam testing your foundational knowledge
4. **Interview:** Attend an interview (if selected based on exam performance)
5. **Final Confirmation:** Receive admission confirmation via email

Each stage is important for selection. Clear instructions and timelines are provided at each step.

## Selection Process

## Course Info / Syllabus

The program covers 6 major AI/ML topics: Generative AI, Machine Learning, Computer Vision, NLP, Neural Networks & Deep Learning, and MLOps.

The fellowship includes comprehensive training across key AI domains. A detailed syllabus is provided after enrollment. The curriculum includes:

**1. Generative AI**
- Large language models (LLMs)
- Image generation
- Prompt engineering

**2. Machine Learning**
- Regression
- Classification
- Clustering
- Performance metrics
- Reinforcement learning

**3. Computer Vision**
- Object detection
- Image segmentation
- Image generation

**4. Natural Language Processing**
- Text extraction
- Named Entity Recognition (NER)
- Sentiment analysis

**5. Neural Networks & Deep Learning**
- Artificial neural networks
- TensorFlow / PyTorch
- Training, evaluating, and fine-tuning models
- CNNs, RNNs, GANs, and SOTA models
- Deep reinforcement learning

**6. MLOps**
- Deployment
- Monitoring
- ML as a Service

Courses include lectures, practical projects, and assessments.

## Duration of Course

The fellowship is 24 weeks long: 21 weeks of coursework plus 3 weeks for project work.

The AI Fellowship program spans a total of 24 weeks (approximately 6 months). The first 21 weeks focus on core learning modules including Generative AI, Machine Learning, Computer Vision, NLP, Neural Networks & Deep Learning, and MLOps. The final 3 weeks are dedicated to completing capstone project work. Classes are held in the evening to accommodate working professionals and students.
